GAP 2: Аня в стране чудес (Q108527991)

We decided to focus on his translations, which are a very important part of his literary output. After finding which of Nabokov's translations appear on Wikidata, we decided to focus on his translation of "Alice's Adventures in Wonderland". We noticed that it missed relevant information, namely publisher, place of publication, and illustrator. We used LLM to find the missing information, and to create RDF triples representing that information.

QUERIES

Description of the Query SPARQL Query
We ran a query to see all items with the property “translator” (P655) and object “Vladimir Nabokov” (Q36591).

RESULTS 

SELECT ?item ?itemLabel
WHERE { 
  ?item wdt:P655 wd:Q36591
  SERVICE wikibase:label {bd:serviceParam wikibase:language "en". } 
}
We ran a query to retrieve information about “place of publication” (P291), “illustrator” (P110) and “publisher” (P123) for all items that have the property “edition or translation of” (P629) and value “Alice’s Adventures in Wonderland” (Q92640).
We used the keyword OPTIONAL so the results would include items that didn’t satisfy the requests about publisher, place, and illustrator.

RESULTS 

SELECT ?item ?itemLabel ?publisher ?publisherLabel ?placeofpublication ?placeofpublicationLabel ?illustrator ?illustratorLabel
WHERE {
  ?item wdt:P629 wd:Q92640 .   
  OPTIONAL { ?item wdt:P123 ?publisher .}  
  OPTIONAL { ?item wdt:P291 ?placeofpublication .}  
  OPTIONAL { ?item wdt:P110 ?illustrator .}  
  SERVICE wikibase:label { bd:serviceParam wikibase:language "[AUTO_LANGUAGE],en". }
}
ORDER BY DESC (?itemLabel)
LIMIT 50

LLM PROMPTING

Both gave us correct and synthetic information regarding the place of publication and the publisher, but two different answers regarding the illustrator. We checked an external source (Wikipedia), and found that the correct answer is Serge Chubine Q4185589 (alias Sergei Zalshupin). This is an example of an AI hallucination.

Question ChatGPT Gemini
Where was Nabokov's translation of Alice in Wonderland published, by what publisher and who was the illustrator? Vladimir Nabokov’s Russian translation of Alice’s Adventures in Wonderland was published in Berlin in 1923 under the title “Аня в стране чудес” (Anya v strane chudes).
• Publisher: Gamaiun (Гамаюн), a Russian émigré press in Berlin.
• Illustrations: by Sergey Sudeikin, the Russian painter and set designer associated with the Ballets Russes. 		Vladimir Nabokov's translation of Alice in Wonderland, titled Ania v strane chudes ("Anya in Wonderland"), was first published in Berlin in 1923.
The publisher was the Russian émigré publishing house Gamaiun.
The illustrations were by Sergei Aleksandrovich Zalshupin, a Russian émigré artist and book illustrator.

We guided ChatGPT towards the correct answer using CHAIN OF THOUGHT PROMPTING.

Q: Sergey Sudeikin was mainly a scenographer and not an illustrator, and he worked mainly in Paris, so could he have illustrated a book published in Berlin? Let's think step by step, considering all the Russian emigre illustrators that were active in Berlin in 1923. You’re absolutely right to be skeptical about Sergei Sudeikin. He was indeed primarily a scenographer and painter, based in Paris by the early 1920s, and there’s no evidence that he worked on Nabokov’s Alice in Berlin.

The illustrator of Nabokov’s 1923 Аня в стране чудес (Anya v strane chudes) was in fact Sergei Aleksandrovich Zalshupin (1898–1931), also known under the pseudonym Serg Choubine (Серж Шубин).

Here are the details step by step:

• Publisher: Издательство Гамаюн (Gamayun), a Russian émigré press in Berlin.

• Year: 1923.

• Illustrator: Sergei Zalshupin, a Russian émigré graphic artist and painter who was active in Berlin at the time (later moved to Paris, where he died young).

• Why not Sudeikin? Sudeikin was by then working mainly as a set designer in Paris (for Diaghilev’s Ballets Russes and later the Metropolitan Opera). He was not involved with Berlin publishing houses of the émigré community.

So the correct bibliographic entry is: Lewis Carroll (pseud. Charles Dodgson). Аня в стране чудес. Перевод В. В. Набокова. Берлин: Гамаюн, 1923. Иллюстрации Сергея Залшупина.
